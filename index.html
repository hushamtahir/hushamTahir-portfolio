Topic 1

Risk Management in Nursing

Risk management refers to the systematic process of identifying, assessing, and reducing risks that may negatively affect patients, staff, or outcomes. It ensures patient safety, quality of care, and reduces adverse events.

Prevents Clinical Errors

Systematic approach reduces mistakes in patient care

Improves Outcomes

Better patient health results through proactive measures

Reduces Costs

Fewer adverse events mean lower healthcare expenses

Promotes Safety Culture

Creates an environment focused on patient wellbeing

Risk Management Steps

1

Identification

Detect potential risks in the clinical environment

2

Analysis

Determine severity and likelihood of each risk

3

Planning

Develop strategies to prevent harmful events

4

Implementation

Apply interventions systematically

5

Evaluation

Monitor changes and improvements

9 Ways Nurses Can Prevent Medical Errors

1

Ask for clarification when unsure

2

Utilize staff and supervisors as resources

3

Complete hourly patient rounding

4

5

Document thoroughly

6

7

8

Topic 2

Measuring Weight & Height

Accurate measurement of height and weight is a basic nursing assessment. These measurements help calculate Body Mass Index (BMI) — a key indicator of nutritional status and health risks.

How to Measure Weight

  1. 1

    Ask the patient to remove shoes & heavy clothes

  2. 2

    Use a calibrated weighing scale

  3. 3

    Ensure the patient stands still

  4. 4

    Record weight precisely

How to Measure Height

  1. 1

    Stand patient against a stadiometer or wall

  2. 2

    Heels, buttocks, and shoulders aligned

  3. 3

    Record height at top of head

  4. 4

    Use same method each time

Calculating BMI

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m²)

Example:
Weight = 70 kg, Height = 1.75 m
BMI = 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.86
(Within normal range)

BMI Categories

< 18.5

Underweight

18.5 - 24.9

Normal

25 - 29.9

Overweight

≥ 30

Obese

BMI Examples

Height Weight BMI Category
1.50 m 45 kg 20 Normal
1.65 m 80 kg 29 Overweight
1.80 m 110 kg 34 Obese

Practical Application in Nursing

  • Accurate height/weight → correct medication dosing
  • BMI → nutritional counselling
  • Risk assessment tools → early detection of complications

Conclusion

Both risk management and accurate anthropometric measurements (weight & height) are essential nursing competencies. They ensure safer, evidence-based care and strong clinical reasoning.
